Welcome to Options Pricing Models

This website provides tools for calculating option prices using two popular models: the Black-Scholes model and the Monte Carlo simulation. These models help investors and traders estimate the fair value of options based on different market conditions and assumptions.

Choose a model below to get started with pricing your options:

Black-Scholes

The Black-Scholes model takes into account of 5 main parameters:

  • Stock Price
  • Strike Price
  • Time to Expiry
  • Volatility
  • Risk-free Interest Rate

This model uses a fairly simple mathematical and deterministic approach towards calculating the price of a call or put option while assuming constant volatility and lognormal asset prices.

Monte Carlo

Monte Carlo simulation takes into account the same 5 parameters as Black-Scholes and an additional 3 parameters.

  • Drift Term
  • Number of Steps
  • Number of Simulation Paths

This model is mathematically complex and computationally intensive as it simulates numerous possible paths the price of an asset may take to estimate the price of an option.
